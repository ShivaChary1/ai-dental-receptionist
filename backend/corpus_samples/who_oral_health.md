# Oral health overview

Oral diseases, while largely preventable, pose a major health burden affecting an estimated 3.5 billion people worldwide. Untreated dental caries in permanent teeth is the most common health condition globally.

# Dental caries

Dental caries results when plaque forms on the surface of a tooth and converts free sugars into acids that destroy the tooth over time. Continued high intake of free sugars, inadequate exposure to fluoride and a lack of removal of plaque by toothbrushing can lead to caries, pain and sometimes tooth loss and infection.

# Periodontal disease

Severe periodontal disease is estimated to affect around 19% of the global adult population. The main risk factors are poor oral hygiene and tobacco use. It presents as bleeding or swollen gums, pain and sometimes bad breath; in severe forms the gum detaches from the tooth and supporting bone, causing teeth to loosen and sometimes fall out.

# Prevention

Oral disease burden can be reduced through: keeping free sugar intake below 10% of total energy; brushing twice daily with fluoride toothpaste; stopping tobacco and areca-nut (gutkha, paan) use; and adequate exposure to fluoride, including fluoridated water where available.
