# Fluoride toothpaste

Brushing twice daily with a fluoride toothpaste is the single most effective self-care measure against dental caries. Adults and children over 6 should use toothpaste with 1350-1500 ppm fluoride. After brushing, spit out excess toothpaste but do not rinse with water, so the fluoride keeps working.

# Children

For children under 3 years, use a smear (rice-grain amount) of fluoride toothpaste; for ages 3-6, a pea-sized amount, supervised so it is not swallowed. Dental fluorosis, common in areas of India with high natural fluoride in groundwater, appears as white flecks or brown mottling and results from excess fluoride ingestion while teeth are developing.

# Professional application

Fluoride varnish (22,600 ppm) applied professionally two or more times a year reduces caries in both primary and permanent teeth. High-risk patients may be prescribed high-fluoride toothpaste by their dentist.
